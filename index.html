Workplace Protection Against Solar UV Radiation

The Sun's Hidden Risk

Excessive and/or accumulated exposure to solar UV radiation causes harmful short-term and long-term effects. Skin cancer is the most common of all cancers, and its incidence has increased dangerously.

Your Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Protective Clothing: Color Matters

Clothing is a fundamental barrier. Dark colors and tightly woven fabrics (like polyester) offer greater protection than light, thin fabrics (like cotton).

Sunscreen (SPF)

Minimum SPF 30: For all exposed workers.

SPF 50+: For areas with snow/water, high altitude, or higher risk.

Application: Every 2-3 hours. Apply 20 minutes before exposure.

Correct amount: Using enough is key. Insufficient application drastically reduces protection.
